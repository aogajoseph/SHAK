HOUSE OF HOPE NAIROBI

House of Hope Nairobi is a "Halfway Home" for persons with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus seeking treatment in hospitals accross Nairobi. Through this initiative, SHAK in partnership with stakeholders, donors and wellwishers provides free meals, accommodation, training on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus, basic rehabilitation and empowerment programs for parents and children.

The House of Hope project was established in 2014 in Kijabe, Kiambu county near the AIC Kijabe, mission hospital. This idea was concieved in response to the challenges that parents and guardians of children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus faced as they sought treatment at Bethany Kids and Kijabe hospitals from different locations.

Usually, the medical interventions for persons with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus require constant hospital calls where surgery dates are random, postsurgical reviews are frequent and continence care should be consistent. For these reasons, parents and guardians of children with Spina bifida encountered countless challenges, regarding accomodation, feeding and travel expenses. Most times, they spent nights along hospital corridors on the floors without food, water and other basic needs. This situation discouraged parents and guardians from seeking medical care for children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus which led to deterioriated conditions and sometimes, death.

In partnership with Child-Help International, SHAK Nairobi has set up locations where such children, parents and guardians are accommodated, fed and trained on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus and engaged on income-generating activities free of charge. Through this program, parents get to interact with one another, share their experiences, renew their hope and receive folic acid supplements to prevent future occurances of Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus.

Child-Help International has played an important role in forming Houses of Hope in Nairobi, Mombasa and Bomet in Kenya; Kitongo, Nyegezi, Zanzibar and Dar es Salaam In Tanzania and others in Uganda, Malawi and Zambia.

Collective action amplifies the strength for change and a better future at SHAK Nairobi. Your involvement is a huge driver in supporting a child with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus. Join us today: Let's make a lasting impact together!
