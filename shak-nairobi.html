THE STORY OF SHAK NAIROBI

SPINABIFIDA and HYDROCEPHALUS ASSOCIATION of KENYA, SHAK - Nairobi is a Community Based Organization registered in 2013 under the Societies Act Cap 108 of the Laws of Kenya to promote health and wellness of persons with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus through Immunization, Advocacy and Publicity and Lifelong care. The Organization was founded by a group of parents who felt the urge to come together and support one another in bringing up children born with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus and caring for them.

In 1996, Juliana Auma, one of SHAK Nairobi's founding members was admitted at the Pumwani Maternity hospital, Nairobi where she delivered a baby girl, whom she named Phoebe Achieng'. Phoebe was born with Spinabifida and Hydrocephalus. Juliana knew very little about these conditions then and embarked on fetching information on them.

In that period, cases of Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus were being recorded in various parts of Kenya but very few people understood the conditions. In her quest for information and medical care, Juliana visited different hospitals countless times before meeting the late Dr. Bransford at Kijabe Mission hospital, Kiambu county.

Other parents of children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus like Juliana encountered more or less similar experiences. They travelled long distances in search of healthcare and information on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus and finally landed at Kijabe hospital. Later on, they met Juliana who shared with them what she had been learning from Dr. Bransford. This gave them hope and changed their perceptions on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus.

These parents developed an interest in learning more about their children's conditions and their roles in caring for them. Their numbers gradually increased as more cases were recorded and parents didn't know where to turn. They started organizing parents' meetings where they discussed advocacy and publicity, lifelong care practices for their children, preventive measures among others.

They started raising awareness through walks, hospital, school and home visits etc., They engaged in different activities for a number of years and finally registered the group as a Community Based Organization (CBO) in 2013.

Today, SHAK Nairobi works with different hospitals in raising awareness, promoting the prevention of Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus and providing lifelong care through the House of Hope project.
