WHAT WE DO

Advocacy, prevention, sensitization and support for people living with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus. through publicity, immunization and lifelong care.

Primary Prevention

Promoting immunization among women of childbearing ages to prevent the occurrence of Spina Bifida. A regular intake of vitamin B-based folic acid supplements and foods rich in folate such as green leafy vegetables, red beans, fresh fruits, sweet potatoes and fortified cereals reduces the risks of neural tube defects in births. This intake should be constant during the first trimester (the first 3 months) of pregnancy.

Lifelong Care

When caregivers of people with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus seek medical care in surgeries, reviews and postsurgical follow-ups, we provide free meals, accommodation, training, rehabilitation and income-generating activities through the House of Hope Project.

Publicity and Advocacy

Educating the public on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus on and offline, formulating policies, advocating for disability rights, to promote social inclusion and eradicate discrimination and stigma. SHAK is currently pushing for mandatory food fortification in Africa in partnership with the Global Alliance for Improved Nutrition (GAIN)
